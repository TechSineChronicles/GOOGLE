      Lotfia El Nady's 107th Birthday

    • Like most young people, Lotfia El Nadi didn’t take no for an answer. In an era when Egyptian women were still fighting for equal rights, Lotfia was dreaming of soaring the big blue sky as a pilot. Whenever she took flying lessons, she would tell her father she was attending a study group–with mom’s approval of course. In 1933, El Nadi became the first Egyptian woman to fly a plane from Cairo to Alexandria, accomplishing her goal and moving the needle for women’s right in her homeland. Our doodle in the Middle East marks her 107th birthday.
