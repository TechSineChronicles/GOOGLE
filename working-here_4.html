Working here

Who keeps our corner of the Internet running? We asked a few Googlers about a day in the life at the Hamina data center.

Mitch Fleming, Data Center Operations

Mitch Fleming tightens a valve coupling that ensures we contain seawater from the Gulf of Finland in the appropriate cooling pipes. Mitch is no stranger to making adjustments. He once designed a two-piece plate to go inside the cooling units to better regulate airflow and save energy when switching the units on and off. “You look at things as open-ended problems. I can basically stop what I'm doing and develop something at will. You are allowed to think outside the box here,” he says.

Julian Cooper, Hardware Operations

Julian Cooper, seen kayaking near the Hamina data center site, works on a team of hardware technicians who maintain and repair Google servers. He starts his day with a cup of coffee before hitting the server floor to start repairing and swapping components. "No two days are the same as we change our roles around frequently to keep things fresh and new."

When asked about his favorite part of the job, Julian talks about the people he works with. "We have a good sense of humour around the office. We have great conversations, and people are always playing pranks on one another. You never know what's going to happen to your desk if you go on holiday or have a birthday."

The Hamina Data Center Team, Ice Fishing Enthusiasts

There's a strong sense of community at the Hamina data center. From time to time, the team enjoys ice fishing in the frozen Gulf of Finland.

Today, we have more than 90 people working on site as computer technicians; mechanical, electrical and water (HVAC) engineers; facilities and grounds maintenance staff; caterers and security personnel. When Phase II of the datacenter is fully operational, we expect to add approximately 25 staff in full-time and contractor roles. Many of the staff are Finnish, and a majority already live in the local community. As we continue building the data center, we will continue to hire locally as much as possible.
