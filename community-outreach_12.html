Mayes County, Oklahoma

In the community

At Google, we’re committed to playing a positive role in each of the communities in which we operate around the world. Through grant-making and local initiatives, we're working to help businesses, schools, and nonprofits thrive. We're always seeking opportunities for Googlers to share their time and expertise with the local community. Here's a sample of what we're doing in the area.

Google's community contributions by the numbers:

$700 million: Our long-term investment in the Mayes County data center facility.

$786,000: Total charitable giving dollars we've donated to schools and nonprofits in Oklahoma since 2010.

Partnership & events

Google works with local organizations to sponsor several community events each year. From promoting science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) education to helping small businesses get online, here are some recent examples of our community involvement:

Aero Games
Google Mayes County hosted its first-ever Aero Games competition in partnership with Rogers State University and the Tulsa Air and Space Museum. More than 150 students competed in the games, which featured gliders, wind powered rockets, remote controlled helicopters, and other wind and aerospace objects.
Google for Veterans launch
In September 2012, coinciding with the return of Oklahoma's 45th Infantry from Afghanistan, Google and Lt. Gov. Todd Lamb announced the Oklahoma launch of Google for Veterans. Designed by the Google Veteran Network (the company’s employee community of veterans, reservists, guardsman, family members and supporters), the site aims to connect veterans with free Google resources to make the transition back into civilian life easier.
Oklahoma Get Your Business Online
As part of a larger effort to get the nation's businesses online, Google hosted seminars in Pryor, Oklahoma City, and Tulsa to educate hundreds of business owners on how to set up and run a successful website. Attendees received an easy-to-build website, customized domain name, free web hosting for a year, and online tools and training. Watch this video to see how we helped get the Pryor Arts Council online in 2011.
GoogleServe
In addition to in-kind giving, local sponsorships and community grants, Google Mayes County employees are active volunteers in the community. For the past two years, employees have participated in Google’s annual GoogleServe project, performing a week of community service throughout the area with organizations including the Cherokee Nation and Pryor Creek Nature Trail.

Community grants

Since 2010, Google has awarded more than $786,000 to Oklahoma nonprofits and schools in areas that we're passionate about. This includes science and technology education, renewable energy innovation, and access to the Internet.

Our grant recipients include:

  • Boys and Girls Clubs of Green Country
  • Chouteau-Mazie Leadership & Academic Support for Students
  • Indian Capital Technology Center Foundation Inc.
  • Pryor Area Foundation
  • Pryor Academic Excellence Foundation
  • Oklahoma State University
  • Pryor Public Schools
  • Locust Grove Schools
  • Safenet Services
  • Thunderbird Youth Academy

When evaluating proposals, we consider:

  • Impact on the data center community or surrounding areas.
  • Measurable impact of the resources on achieving the mission.
  • Collaboration with other organizations and the ability to leverage other resources in the community.
  • Integration of Google in-kind or volunteer resources to maximize the impact.

Google community grants range from $5,000 to $25,000, and are intended for nonprofit and certain public organizations. We will not fund for-profit companies through this program.

If you believe you have a project or program that fits these guidelines, please register and submit your proposal through our online application. Application deadlines fall on midnight Pacific time on January 31 and June 30.

Green initiatives

Google is also committed to creating a better web that's better for the environment. In 2011, we agreed to purchase all of the energy from NextEra Energy Resources’ Minco II wind facility in Oklahoma for the next 20 years. This 100.8 megawatt facility was built as a direct result of our financial commitment and became operational in late 2011.

We welcome proposals from data center communities that reduce carbon emissions in the immediate region, including projects that increase the renewable energy mix powering our data centers. Applicants can be nonprofit or for-profit entrepreneurs. We’ll be looking for cost of generation, innovation, and total carbon reduction. You can submit proposals via email to mayescounty@google.com.
