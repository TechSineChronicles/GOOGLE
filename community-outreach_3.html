Council Bluffs, Iowa

In the community

At Google, we’re committed to playing a positive role in each of the communities in which we operate around the world. Through grant-making and local initiatives, we're working to help businesses, schools, and nonprofits thrive. We're always seeking opportunities for Googlers to share their time and expertise with the local community. Here's a sample of what we're doing in the area.

Google's community contributions by the numbers:

$2.5 billion: Our long-term investment in the Council Bluffs data center facility.

$820,000: Total charitable giving dollars we've donated to nonprofits, schools, and organizations with an impact in Iowa since 2009.

$204,000: Google WiFi grant awarded to the City of Council Bluffs to help implement a free downtown WiFi network.

50%: Percent less energy consumed at Google facilities, compared to a typical data center.

Partnership & events

Google works with local organizations to sponsor several community events each year. From promoting science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) education to helping small businesses get online, here are some recent examples of our community involvement:

Anti-Gravity Games
Google hosted the first-annual Anti-Gravity Games rocket competition at Iowa Western Community College in 2012. Teams from eleven local schools participated. The winning school received a $5,000 grant for physics lab equipment, and the overall competition winners received Nexus 7 tablets. In addition, a Google employee set a new Guinness World Record for launching 45 potatoes in under three minutes with a homemade potato cannon.
Community WiFi announcement
In 2011, Google partnered with the City of Council Bluffs to roll out the Omaha metropolitan area's first free public WiFi. Council Bluffs now provides free WiFi access to anyone in and around five areas of the city: downtown and 100 Broadway District, Mid-America Center, Harvey Recreational Complex, River’s Edge Park, and City Hall.
Iowa Get Your Business Online
As part of a larger effort to get the nation's businesses online, Google hosted seminars in Iowa cities, including Pella, Des Moines, and Council Bluffs, to educate business owners on how to set up and run a successful website. Attendees received an easy-to-build website, customized domain name, free web hosting for a year, and online tools and training. Representative Brandenburg and Senator Gronstal kicked off the 2012 event in Council Bluffs to support getting Iowa businesses online.
GoogleServe
Since 2010, employees at the data center have participated in Google’s annual GoogleServe project, performing a week of community service throughout the area with local organizations. In 2012, Googlers cleaned and refurbished computer labs, re-imaged and installed computer equipment, and inventoried equipment at Council Bluffs high schools, as seen in the photo here.

Community grants

Since 2009, Google has awarded more than $820,000 to Iowa nonprofits and schools in areas that we're passionate about. This includes science and technology education, renewable energy innovation, and access to the Internet.

Our grant recipients include:

  • Community Education Foundation Inc.
  • Girl Scouts of Greater Iowa
  • Pearson Lakes Arts Center
  • Community Education Foundation Inc.
  • Lewis Central Community School District Foundation
  • AIM Institute
  • Council Bluffs Community School District
  • Iowa Western Community College

When evaluating proposals, we consider:

  • Impact on the data center community or surrounding areas.
  • Measurable impact of the resources on achieving the mission.
  • Collaboration with other organizations and the ability to leverage other resources in the community.
  • Integration of Google in-kind or volunteer resources to maximize the impact.

Google community grants range from $5,000 to $25,000, and are intended for nonprofit and certain public organizations. We will not fund for-profit companies through this program.

If you believe you have a project or program that fits these guidelines, please register and submit your proposal through our online application. Application deadlines fall on midnight Pacific time on January 31 and June 30.

Green initiatives

Google is also committed to creating a better web that's better for the environment. Through a 20-year green Power Purchase Agreement, we've purchased 114 MW of wind generation from a facility in Story County, and we've invested $75 million in a 50 MW wind farm in Greene County. Most recently, we signed an agreement with our utility partner, MidAmercan Energy, to supply our Council Bluffs data center with up to 407 MW of wind energy from several wind projects built as part of MidAmerica's Wind VIII program. Together, our investments and agreements help us foster true growth in the renewable energy sector.

We welcome proposals from data center communities that reduce carbon emissions in the immediate region, including projects that increase the renewable energy mix powering our data centers. Applicants can be nonprofit or for-profit entrepreneurs. We’ll be looking for cost of generation, innovation, and total carbon reduction. You can submit proposals via email to councilbluffs@google.com.
