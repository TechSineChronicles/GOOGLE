Working here

Who keeps our corner of the Internet running? We asked a few Googlers about a day in the life at the Dublin data center.

Thomas Heffernan, Hardware Operations

Tom works on a team responsible for keeping the servers up and running. He focuses on diagnosing and fixing any issues that arise within the network. "One of the main things I like about my job is the vast amount of new technologies that I work with on a daily basis," he says. "New projects come thick and fast. As soon as you've finished one, there's another to look forward to."

Daria Smerd, Program Manager

A program manager on the Hardware Operations team, Daria ensures that all machine deployments, such as upgrades are executed as planned. Although she is based in Ireland, her work covers projects in data centers around the world: "Working with teams from different timezones sets the structure of each day at work—from working with the Asian and European data center technicians in the morning to syncing up with US data centers in the afternoon and finishing the day with meeting planners in Mountain View, Google's headquarters in California."

John Wiltshire, Data Center Operations

As a data center operations technician, John maintains the integrity of the power and cooling for the data center. His job also includes overall maintenance of the facility, including its safety systems, security systems, light, power, and air conditioning. He describes the culture at the Dublin data center as close-knit: "You are going into a work place that has great people that you can have some good 'craic' (or enjoyable conversation) with, but you know that they are also extremely competent and reliable. This makes the job truly enjoyable."

Philip Davis, Hardware Operations

With a passion for fixing anything that needs fixing, from cars to old arcade machines, Philip manages the team responsible for keeping Google servers up and running 24/7. A typical day starts with a team sync on what needs to be done throughout the day and includes an afternoon tea break where everyone regroups for tea, biscuits, and general catching up. The favorite part of his job? "Getting to work with technologies that I never thought I would ever see or get to play with—technologies that haven't even reached the market yet."
